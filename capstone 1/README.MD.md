**Business Scenario**

I am working as a Data Analyst for EmporiUm, a student bookstore chain that sells books, tech products, and art supplies both in-store and online Sales are organized by:

Region (State-level) Sales Territory (group of stores or online areas)



A sales manager requested an analysis of a specific sales territory to better understand performance and guide decisions for the upcoming quarter.



**objectives** 

&#x20;- Analyze sales performance for New York

&#x20;- Identify trends and patterns in revenue

&#x20;- Compare territory performance to the broader region

&#x20;- Provide recommendations to improve future sales

**Data source** 

&#x20; the data is exported from data base called sample sales which include online and store sales, store location, products ,shipper list and others. the  analysis is based on those tables.



**Analysis and key questions** 

In this analysis, I looked at how the sales territory is performing by answering a few key questions. First, I calculated the total revenue and checked the time period to understand overall sales. Then, I looked at revenue by month to see if there were any trends or patterns. I also compared the territory’s revenue to the overall New York region to see how it performs compared to others. After that, I analyzed the number of transactions and average sale amount by product to find which products are doing well. Finally, I ranked the stores based on revenue to see which ones are performing the best and which ones might need improvement.



**Recommendations**

Based on the analysis I recommend focusing on improving lower-performing stores like 851 and 844 by reviewing factors such as customer traffic and inventory. High-performing stores like 850 and 847 should be used as examples of what is working well.



Top-selling products, such as 105248-IT and 105250-IT, should be promoted and kept in stock. In addition, online sales should be analyzed and improved, especially in states with lower performance, by increasing marketing and customer reach.



Finally, monthly sales trends should be used to better plan inventory, staffing, and marketing for the next quarter.
